## [[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]] Under the Mask

[[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]] continued its performance of forced joy.

Every street carried the same contradiction: smiles painted over fear, festival banners hanging beside gallows. Punishment for “malicious unhappiness” remained public and theatrical. The town demanded celebration not because its people were joyful, but because they were terrified not to appear so.

Even now, beneath the noise and rituals, something rotten pressed against the surface.

We had no time to dwell on it.

The bones of Saint Andral were still missing.

---

## Returning to the Church

We returned to St. Andral’s Church after learning more about the theft. Father Lucian remained desperate to recover the relics before the church lost its protection entirely.

Milivoj — strained, frightened, and near collapse — finally admitted enough for us to follow the trail toward the coffin maker, Hendrik van der Voort.

The situation surrounding Milivoj became clearer the longer we dealt with him: not malicious, only cornered. Barovia creates desperation first, then punishes people for surviving incorrectly.

That realization did little to improve our odds.

We armed ourselves regardless.

Wooden stakes. Holy water. Improvised plans.

Preparation born more from necessity than confidence.

---

## Hendrik’s House

The coffin maker’s home stood silent and diseased, as though the structure itself knew what slept inside it.

Hendrik confirmed our suspicions almost immediately. Terrified beyond reason, he revealed that vampire spawn were hidden upstairs among the coffins.

He wanted us gone as much as he wanted them gone.

We considered burning the entire building.

Instead, we climbed the stairs.

---

## The Ambush Upstairs

Father Lucian accompanied us despite every indication that he should not have.

We attempted stealth.

It failed almost immediately.

A loose plank betrayed us. Coffins shifted open in the darkness. Pale figures began unfolding themselves from inside the boxes with unnatural speed.

Combat erupted before the room had fully awakened.

Brennick charged first, forcing one of the spawn back into its coffin while driving radiant power through his strikes. Hugh and Orianna moved quickly to contain the room before the creatures could overwhelm us.

And then another figure entered the fight.

---

## The Woman in the Window

Until that moment, I had only observed her from a distance.

A woman moved through the broken window with terrifying precision — fast, controlled, utterly focused. Dark hair. Hooded cloak. Twin daggers coated in poison.

She did not hesitate.

Where Brennick fought like a soldier and Hugh like a storm, she fought like execution itself.

One of the spawn barely had time to react before she buried a blade directly through its heart.

The violence was efficient. Intimate. Practiced.

I remember noticing something strange in that moment:

The vampires did not fight as creatures accustomed to pain.

They fought as creatures accustomed to invulnerability.

And for the first time that night, they realized they could die.

---

## The Ritual Circle

As the fighting continued, I noticed something else.

A magical circle burned into the floor beneath the coffins.

Teleportation magic.

Not dormant — active.

The energy was building rapidly.

I tried to disrupt it, but whatever force powered the circle was already too far along.

Then the stone reacted.

Not in my possession — in Van’s.

I felt the resonance immediately, like tension pulling through the Weave itself.

And then came the voice.

Not heard through sound.

Felt.

> *Use me.*

The sensation was invasive and immediate. Persuasive in a way that frightened me far more than open malice would have.

But I did not obey it.

Instead, I realized the stone was connected to whatever was arriving through the portal.

I shouted for Van to get away from the circle immediately.

He did not hesitate.

Van leapt through the window with the stone while Father Lucian fled downstairs toward the street. Moments later, Father Lucian heard a woman’s voice from inside the room — calm, controlled, aristocratic.

Van met him outside and forced the stone into his hands.

Then he ordered the priest back to the church.

The portal completed before any of us could stop it.

And someone stepped through.

---

## Anastasia

A woman appeared within the circle dressed like nobility from another age.

Calm. Pale. Composed.

Dangerous.

She identified herself as Anastasia Karelova — one of Strahd’s brides.

Not merely a vampire.

Something older. Sharper.

The room changed the moment she arrived. Even Brennick, who had stood firm against the spawn moments earlier, understood immediately that this was not the same kind of threat.

Yet she did not attack us.

Instead, she asked questions.

And somehow, impossibly, the conversation became negotiation rather than slaughter.

---

## The Bargain

Anastasia confirmed what we already suspected:

She possessed the bones.

But she refused to return them immediately.

According to her, removing the bones served some larger purpose connected to Strahd’s interests in Vallaki. Yet unlike the other horrors we have encountered, she spoke with restraint — even reason.

More unsettling still:

She seemed genuinely concerned about the orphanage.

The same orphanage connected to the dream pastries.

The same orphanage where children disappear.

She proposed a bargain.

If we investigated the hags tied to the orphanage and brought her information about their activities, she would ensure the bones could be recovered safely afterward.

Not a command.

Not a threat.

A negotiation.

I still do not know whether that makes her more dangerous or less.

---

## Uneasy Understanding

The discussion lasted far longer than it should have.

Long enough to realize something uncomfortable:

Anastasia was not pretending to be human.

She understood perfectly well what she was.

And yet, unlike every other servant of Strahd we have encountered, she seemed capable of choosing restraint.

I do not trust her.

But I believe she was truthful.

Those are not the same thing.

---

## Return to the Church

We eventually returned to Father Lucian and explained what we could without revealing everything.

He struggled to accept the truth about Anastasia. Understandably so. To him, a vampire with restraint is almost a contradiction in terms.

Still, we convinced him of one thing:

The bones are recoverable.

And we intend to retrieve them.

For now, that is enough to keep panic from consuming the church entirely.

---

## The Stone

Later, after we returned to the church, Van retrieved the stone back from Father Lucian and took it outside alone into the graveyard.

I watched from a distance.

The stone had already proven itself capable of responding to magical phenomena around it — specifically the teleportation circle. Whatever intelligence or force inhabits it is neither dormant nor mindless.

Van attempted to force a reaction from it by channeling radiant energy into the stone directly.

Nothing happened.

At least physically.

The stone simply answered with the same voice as before.

Calm. Mocking.

Untouched.

> *That will not work.*

No pain. No resistance. No sign the radiant power had affected it in the slightest.

That disturbed me more than if it had retaliated.

Artifacts that fear destruction can be understood.

This one sounded amused by the attempt.

---

## Reflections

Tonight changed something.

Not because we fought vampires.

That part was expected.

What changed was realizing Barovia is more complicated than monsters and victims.

Some monsters negotiate.

Some priests panic.

Some thieves are only starving boys.

And some voices inside ancient stones sound dangerously convincing.

I do not know yet which possibility frightens me most.
