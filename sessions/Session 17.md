## [[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]] Under the Mask

[[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]] continued its performance of forced joy. Every street carried the same contradiction—smiles painted over fear, festival banners hanging beside gallows. Punishment for *malicious unhappiness* remained public and theatrical: the town demanded celebration not because its people were joyful, but because they were terrified not to appear so. Beneath the noise, something rotten pressed steadily against the surface. We had no time to dwell on it. The bones of Saint Andral were still missing.

---

## Returning to the Church

We returned to [[St. Andral's Church]] after learning more about the theft. [[Father Lucian]] remained desperate to recover the relics before the church's protection failed entirely. [[Milivoj]]—strained, frightened, and near collapse—at last admitted enough for us to follow the trail toward the coffin maker, [[Hendrik|Hendrik van der Voort]]. The situation around [[Milivoj]] became clearer the longer we dealt with him: not malicious, only cornered. [[The Land of Barovia|Barovia]] creates desperation first, then punishes people for surviving it incorrectly. That realisation did little to improve our odds.

We armed ourselves regardless—wooden stakes, holy water, improvised plans. Preparation born more from necessity than from confidence.

---

## [[Hendrik]]'s House

The coffin maker's home stood silent and diseased, as though the structure itself knew what slept inside it. [[Hendrik]] confirmed our suspicions almost immediately; terrified beyond reason, he revealed that [[Vampiric Entities|vampire]] spawn were hidden upstairs among the coffins. He wanted us gone as much as he wanted them gone. We considered burning the entire building, and discarded the idea. Instead we climbed the stairs.

---

## The Ambush Upstairs

[[Father Lucian]] accompanied us despite every indication that he should not have. We attempted stealth; it failed almost immediately. A loose plank betrayed us, coffins shifted open in the darkness, and pale figures began unfolding themselves from inside the boxes with unnatural speed. Combat erupted before the room had fully awakened.

[[Brennick]] charged first, forcing one of the spawn back into its coffin while driving radiant power through his strikes. [[Hugh]] and [[Orianna]] moved quickly to contain the room before the creatures could overwhelm us. And then another figure entered the fight.

---

## The Woman in the Window

Until that moment, I had only observed her from a distance. She moved through the broken window with terrifying precision—fast, controlled, utterly focused. Dark hair, hooded cloak, twin daggers coated in poison. She did not hesitate. Where [[Brennick]] fought like a soldier and [[Hugh]] like a storm, she fought like execution itself; one of the spawn barely had time to react before she buried a blade directly through its heart. The violence was efficient, intimate, practised.

I remember noticing something strange in that moment. The [[Vampiric Entities|vampires]] did not fight as creatures accustomed to pain. They fought as creatures accustomed to invulnerability—and for the first time that night, they realised they could die.

---

## The Ritual Circle

As the fighting continued, I noticed something else: a magical circle burned into the floor beneath the coffins. Teleportation magic, and not dormant—active, with its energy building rapidly. I tried to disrupt it, but whatever force powered the circle was already too far along.

Then the [[The Stone|stone]] reacted—not in my possession, in [[Van]]'s. I felt the resonance immediately, like tension pulling through the Weave itself, and a voice followed: not heard through sound, but *felt*.

> *Use me.*

The sensation was invasive and immediate, persuasive in a way that frightened me far more than open malice would have. I did not obey it. Instead I realised the [[The Stone|stone]] was connected to whatever was arriving through the portal, and shouted for [[Van]] to get away from the circle at once. He did not hesitate. He leapt through the window with the [[The Stone|stone]] while [[Father Lucian]] fled downstairs toward the street. Moments later [[Father Lucian]] heard a woman's voice from inside the room—calm, controlled, aristocratic. [[Van]] met him outside, forced the [[The Stone|stone]] into his hands, and ordered the priest back to the church.

The portal completed before any of us could stop it. And someone stepped through.

---

## [[Anastasia Karelova|Anastasia]]

A woman appeared within the circle dressed like nobility from another age—calm, pale, composed, dangerous. She identified herself as [[Anastasia Karelova]], one of [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s brides. Not merely a [[Vampiric Entities|vampire]]; something older, sharper. The room changed the moment she arrived: even [[Brennick]], who had stood firm against the spawn moments earlier, understood immediately that this was not the same kind of threat. Yet she did not attack us. She asked questions instead, and somehow, impossibly, the conversation became negotiation rather than slaughter.

---

## The Bargain

[[Anastasia Karelova|Anastasia]] confirmed what we already suspected—she possessed the bones—but she refused to return them immediately. According to her, removing them served some larger purpose connected to [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s interests in [[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]]. Unlike the other horrors we have encountered, she spoke with restraint, even reason. More unsettling still, she seemed genuinely concerned about the orphanage—the same orphanage connected to the dream pastries, the same orphanage where children disappear.

She proposed a bargain: if we investigated the [[Hags|hags]] tied to the orphanage and brought her information about their activities, she would ensure the bones could be recovered safely afterward. Not a command. Not a threat. A negotiation. I still do not know whether that makes her more dangerous or less.

---

## Uneasy Understanding

The discussion lasted far longer than it should have—long enough to realise something uncomfortable. [[Anastasia Karelova|Anastasia]] was not pretending to be human; she understood perfectly well what she was. And yet, unlike every other servant of [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] we have encountered, she seemed capable of choosing restraint. I do not trust her, but I believe she was truthful. Those are not the same thing.

---

## Return to the Church

We eventually returned to [[Father Lucian]] and explained what we could without revealing everything. He struggled to accept the truth about [[Anastasia Karelova|Anastasia]]—understandably so; to him, a [[Vampiric Entities|vampire]] with restraint is almost a contradiction in terms. Still, we convinced him of one thing: the bones are recoverable, and we intend to retrieve them. For now, that is enough to keep panic from consuming the church entirely.

---

## The Stone

Later, after we returned to the church, [[Van]] retrieved the [[The Stone|stone]] back from [[Father Lucian]] and took it outside alone into the graveyard. I watched from a distance. The [[The Stone|stone]] had already proven itself capable of responding to magical phenomena around it—specifically the teleportation circle—so whatever intelligence or force inhabits it is neither dormant nor mindless. [[Van]] attempted to force a reaction from it by channelling radiant energy into the [[The Stone|stone]] directly.

Nothing happened. At least, nothing physical. The [[The Stone|stone]] simply answered with the same voice as before—calm, mocking, untouched.

> *That will not work.*

No pain, no resistance, no sign the radiant power had affected it in the slightest. That disturbed me more than if it had retaliated. Artefacts that fear destruction can be understood; this one sounded amused by the attempt.

---

## Reflections

Tonight changed something, and not because we fought [[Vampiric Entities|vampires]]—that part was expected. What changed was the recognition that [[The Land of Barovia|Barovia]] is more complicated than monsters and victims. Some monsters negotiate. Some priests panic. Some thieves are only starving boys. And some voices inside ancient stones sound dangerously convincing.

I do not know yet which of those possibilities frightens me most.
