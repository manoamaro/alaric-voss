The raven, once questioned in earnest, did speak. [[Brennick]] handled the interrogation. He has a manner that combines authority with patience in roughly the right proportion, and the bird, after some preliminary resistance, gave us a name and a story.

[[Muriel]]. From the [[The Winery|winery]], or attached to it. She had seen our party on the road and concluded, not unreasonably given recent events, that we were in [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s service. We persuaded her, with patience and with some effort, that we were not. She is no friend of the [[Vampiric Entities|Vampire]]. She wishes the land free, in whatever sense that word can still apply here.

She also gave us a name I had not yet heard.

[[Baba Lysaga]].

A hag, she said, in alliance with the [[Vampiric Entities|Vampire]]. The Arboran [[Arcane Constructs|Construct]] we had destroyed on the road was, according to her, of [[Baba Lysaga]]'s making—built to hunt and destroy her own kin.

That detail bears repeating. The construct was not aimed at us. It was aimed at her.

---

[[Muriel]] gave us, in passing, an explanation of the [[Vistani]] that I had been groping toward without quite reaching: they are the people who can leave the land, with [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]]'s permission. [[Madame Eva]]'s camp is a [[Vistani]] camp. The raven, in her way, is one of them as well.

That changed our plan.

We would return to the [[Vistani]] camp at [[Tser Pool]], taking the raven with us.

Before setting out, I performed [[spells/Level 2/Augury|Augury]] on the proposal. The omen returned favourable. The plan would, in some measure, work.

[[Brennick]], who was nearby during the ritual, reported afterward that he had perceived—through whatever sense his order has cultivated in him—a divine presence, an angelic figure, attending the casting. He described it without ornament. I record it without judgement. The Weave is behaving strangely in this land, and I am no longer in a position to dismiss any report.

---

The camp received us as the camp tends to do: without surprise. [[Madame Eva]]'s tent stood as before, and she received us with the same composure she had shown me on the morning I arrived.

Behind her, on the cloth that formed the rear wall of the tent, was a symbol I had not noticed during my first visit.

I tried to identify it and failed. My Arcana check came up short; I have rarely been more annoyed by a die. [[Brennick]], however, succeeded where I did not. He recognised it at once.

A symbol of [[Shar]].

I will not pretend the implication is small. I will record only that it is being noted, and that I will return to it.

---

While we were still inside the tent, two others arrived.

[[Elysa]] and [[Turi]]. New to me, though plainly not new to the camp. [[Elysa]] offered, without prompting, to assist in our hunt. [[Turi]] said little. I will set down impressions of both of them properly once I have observed each in something other than a doorway.

For my part, I gave [[Madame Eva]] an account of who I am, where I had come from, and how I had arrived. She listened in the way she always listens, which is to say as though the story were not new to her, and might never have been.

[[Stanimir]], for his part, returned the favour. He told us his own story—or, more precisely, he showed it. He cast an illusion of a great map, suspended above the centre of the tent, large enough that we could walk around it. The detail was extraordinary. I had not previously credited him with that order of magic. I shall need to reconsider his measure.

---

Before we departed, two practical matters were settled.

[[Madame Eva]] gave us a [[Scroll of Clairvoyance]]. I have not yet decided who among us should keep it. It is the sort of object whose owner ought to be chosen carefully.

[[Elysa]], as I noted above, offered to join the hunt.

We took the opportunity for a short rest before setting out again. I used the interval to recover the spells I had spent—my [[Arcane Recovery]] is becoming, by quiet degrees, the most valuable habit I possess in this country.

I close the entry here. Tomorrow we move again, and I expect tomorrow to demand more of us than today did.

The day is already too full of new names.
