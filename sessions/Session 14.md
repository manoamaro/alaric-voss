We resumed our journey toward Vallaki with incomplete knowledge and insufficient certainty—an increasingly common condition in this land.

## On Maps and Unreliable Guidance

Attempts were made to acquire a proper map.

They failed.

Barovia does not require maps, it seems. Its inhabitants navigate by memory, habit, or resignation. Directions were given verbally—fragmented, imprecise:

- A wooded stretch
- A gate we had already seen
- A junction toward the castle
- A lake
- A settlement beyond

Useful in theory. Dangerous in practice

I summoned my familiar at first light—a crow, though altered by this place. Its form appeared… wrong. Skeletal edges beneath living shape. The Weave here is tainted; even simple conjurations reflect it.

The others noticed.

They suspect necromancy.

They are not entirely wrong to question.

### Warnings Before Departure

A final warning was given before we left:

Do not follow cries. Do not trust what calls from the mist.

Sound advice. Likely insufficient.

We also concealed the truth regarding a missing apprentice—Elisa. Deception came easily to some among us. The man believed it.

This land rewards omission.

#### Ambush on the Mountain Road

The terrain shifted as we traveled:

- Narrow road
- Steep incline
- Ravine below (~50 feet)

The environment itself was a weapon.

We were ambushed.

Wolves—coordinated, deliberate, intelligent beyond expectation.

Their tactics were precise:

- Target positioning near the edge
- Repeated attempts to force us into the ravine
- Reinforcements waiting below

This was not instinct.


This was learned behavior.

### The Fall

I misjudged the danger.


One wolf seized my leg and pulled.

  

The fall was immediate.

  

No time to react.

  

Impact severe—enough to end consciousness.

  

I recall nothing between the moment of falling and the sensation of forced breath returning to my lungs.

  

Brennick descended and restored me through divine means. His magic remains… altered. Functional, but not pure.

  

I owe him my life.

  

⸻

  

Aftermath of Violence

  

Upon regaining awareness, I acted without restraint.

  

I cast Mind Spike.

  

Not as a calculation—but as anger.

  

The effect was… excessive.

  

The wolf’s mind collapsed under the force. Instant termination.

  

This is worth noting.

  

Emotional casting yields different results. Less efficient, perhaps—but more decisive.

  

⸻

  

Observations on the Wolves

  

Post-engagement analysis:

  

- They coordinated positioning
- They anticipated movement
- They used terrain deliberately
- They avoided Ireena

  

This last point is critical.

  

They could have targeted her. They did not.

  

Conclusion:

These were not ordinary beasts.

  

⸻

  

The Dead Below

  

At the base of the ravine, we found a body.

  

- Human
- Partially consumed
- Likely one of the refugees fleeing recent events

  

No valuables. No identifying marks.

  

Only confirmation:

  

The wolves hunt this road repeatedly.

  

An ambush site.

  

⸻

  

Short Rest and Recovery

  

We withdrew from the road to recover.

  

- Wounds treated
- Fire lit
- Food prepared

  

Morale stabilized, briefly.

  

I examined the recovered spellbook.

  

Through Identify, I forced comprehension:

  

- The script reorganized itself under observation
- Several spells can be transcribed into my own notation
- The methodology is foreign—structured, but alien

  

This reinforces a pattern:

Magic in Barovia is not merely different—it is distorted at a foundational level.

  

⸻

  

The Mist and the Loop

  

Later, the mist thickened.

  

Auditory anomaly:

  

- Footsteps behind us
- Matching our pace
- No visible source

  

Visual anomaly followed:

  

- Figures ahead
- Identical to our party
- Mimicking posture and equipment

  

I identified the likely mechanism:

  

A spatial loop construct—arcane in nature, possibly mist-bound.

  

We confirmed it by marking the ground and observing repetition.

  

We were not advancing.

  

We were contained.

  

⸻

  

Exit Strategy

  

We reversed direction.

  

Key observations:

  

- Footsteps adjusted dynamically
- Loop persisted
- A specific location triggered recognition

  

At that point, we discovered:

  

- A set of Tarokka cards
- A map fragment indicating “Master’s Mercy”

  

No active magic on the objects themselves. The mist carried the enchantment.

  

We chose to leave the road.

  

The mist receded as we advanced into the forest.

  

Conclusion:

The road was the trap. The forest was the exit.

  

⸻

  

Return to the Road

  

We regained the path near dusk.

  

Conditions:

  

- Visibility reduced
- Moisture increasing
- Sound dampened

  

Ahead: a bridge.

  

A figure stood upon it.

  

Still. Armed. Waiting.

  

Not a statue.

  

Not alive.

  

Something else.

  

⸻

  

Preliminary Assessment

  

Brennick confirmed it first:

  

- Undead presence
- Strong aura
- No immediate hostility

  

Likely classification:

  

Revenant

  

A being bound by oath beyond death.

  

Purpose-driven. Persistent. Dangerous.

  

But not inherently hostile.

  

⸻

  

Conclusion

  

Key learnings:

  

- Terrain in Barovia is as lethal as its inhabitants
- Creatures exhibit intelligence beyond expectation
- The mist is not environmental—it is active
- Magic behaves unpredictably, but not without structure
- Death is not always an ending

  

We proceed toward the bridge.

  

Caution remains insufficient.

  

But it is all we have.
