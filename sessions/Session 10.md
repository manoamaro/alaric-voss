I slept poorly. That is becoming a pattern I will need to address before it becomes a condition.

I woke early in [[Madame Eva]]'s camp at [[Tser Pool]], restless and not entirely persuaded that the previous night's visions had ended when I opened my eyes. Outside the tent, [[Stanimir]] greeted me again. He asked, almost in passing, about [[Neverwinter]]—a city he claimed never to have heard of.

I did not press the point. In a land where a man can wake at a crossroads he did not choose, the absence of a city from one stranger's knowledge is a small mystery.

I recounted my conversation with [[Madame Eva]]. He listened without surprise and offered only one piece of counsel: *"Then follow her words."*

After breakfast, I set off toward the crossroads.

---

The walk took several hours. The road behaved as roads should, which by itself was a relief.

At the crossroads I found the gallows—changed.

Four bodies hung where none had stood the day before. An old man in armour, smiling faintly. A second knight in silver trim, his face set in something nearer to fury. A blue-green [[Tieflings|tiefling]] woman. A bronze [[Dragonborns|dragonborn]]. I recognised at once, by the texture of the air around them, that this was another vision; but the recognition did not protect me from it. I approached, and met an invisible barrier that struck me back hard enough to draw blood (–1 HP). When I rose, the bodies were gone.

![[crossroads_gallows_vision.jpg]]

I committed the scene to my notebook before continuing. Visions of the hanged are not the sort one trusts to memory.

---

I continued toward the [[Village of Barovia]].

The smell reached me before the gate did—smoke, and beneath it the unmistakable scent of burning flesh. Villagers were tending a bonfire on which corpses were being consumed in the methodical, exhausted manner of people who have done this work before. I made out armed figures among them and, without ostentation, cast [[spells/Level 1/Mage Armor|Mage Armor]] before proceeding (–1 spell slot).

![[village_barovia_entrance.jpg]]

The streets were mud and silence. Many houses stood plainly abandoned. In the village square a brazier burned brightly, more ceremonial than useful, and beyond it I saw four travellers moving with urgency toward a church.

The four from the gallows.

I followed.

---

A baker's window distracted me on the way: the sweet smell of pastries, almost aggressive in a town that otherwise smelled of fire and damp. The woman at the counter, [[Bella]], offered me one. I refused at first and then, against better judgment, accepted a few to examine later. There are old reasons not to eat food pressed too eagerly upon a stranger, and I intended to honour them.

---

A commotion broke from inside the church.

I reached the door just as a group emerged from a stairwell that clearly led to a basement: a paladin, a fighter, a [[Tieflings|tiefling]] druid, and a bronze [[Dragonborns|dragonborn]]. The four from my vision.

The paladin seized me by the robe before I could speak. *"Show your teeth!"* he demanded. I complied. He released me. The fighter then asked for my name. I gave it, and added, with more composure than I felt, that [[Madame Eva]] had told me to find answers here.

The [[Dragonborns|dragonborn]] was holding a corpse. The fighter—[[Van]]—muttered something about another like them, and told me to stay close. Inside, I learned the dead man had been [[Doru]], son of [[Father Donovich|Priest Donavich]]. The encounter then settled into introductions: the paladin was [[Brennick]], the [[Dragonborns|dragonborn]] [[Hugh]], and there were two others with them—[[Ireena Kolyana|Ireena]] and her brother [[Ismark Kolyanovich|Ismark]]—who had not appeared in my vision at all. That absence is worth noting. The vision had been specific.

[[Hugh]] asked for a pastry. I warned him; he ate it anyway. In return, he handed me a small black orb, claiming it granted visions. I detected no magic on it by touch alone, and began to cast [[spells/Level 1/Detect Magic|Detect Magic]], but [[Van]] interrupted before the spell completed, insisting we move on. I stored the orb for later examination.

---

At [[Bildrath's Mercantile]] I bought a healing potion. The group spoke of travelling to [[Village Vallaki]], and I offered to guide them, having walked the road from [[Stanimir]]'s camp the day before. Rain began as we set out. We took a short rest along the way before continuing.

![[short_rest.jpg]]

Conversation during the journey drifted, as such conversations will, toward undead and [[Vampiric Entities|vampires]]. I have read enough at the [[Neverwinter Academy]] to recognise the patterns being described, and what I heard confirmed, with unwelcome efficiency, several of my suspicions about the curse on this region.

---

At the crossroads again, the gallows stood empty. When [[Van]] asked what had caught my interest, I deflected, directing his attention instead toward the road leading to [[Madame Eva]]'s camp.

Then the mist gathered—thick, fast, and clearly not natural.

A shape moved within it. A black carriage drawn by two dark horses draped in black cloth. The group halted. The driver was a pale servant who introduced himself as [[Escher Belasco|Escher]]. The carriage stopped beside us, and from within stepped [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Lord Strahd von Zarovich]].

![[carriage_strahd_encounter.jpg]]

He is tall, pale, and unhurried in the way of those who have never in their lives needed to be quick. His voice was calm and entirely without warmth.

He remarked that my face was unfamiliar. *"You were not announced,"* he said. He then turned his attention to [[Ireena Kolyana|Ireena]] and called her a servant, plainly to provoke. [[Brennick]] and [[Van]] stepped between him and her. He warned them, mildly, against meddling in his affairs.

Then he demanded a demonstration from each of us.

[[Brennick]]'s blessed sword bit him in a way he had clearly not anticipated. He acknowledged the weapon with something between curiosity and respect. When I cast [[spells/Level 0/Fire Bolt|Fire Bolt]] he was visibly unimpressed. I tried [[spells/Level 2/Mind Spike|Mind Spike]] on [[Escher Belasco|Escher]]; the spell connected but did almost no harm. [[Count Strahd von Zarovich|Strahd]] smiled faintly. *"Weak, but curious,"* he said.

He returned to the carriage and rode on into the mist.

---

We resumed the road toward [[Village Vallaki|Vallaki]].

We had not gone far when a raven dropped out of the sky onto the path ahead. It was alive, but it should not have been—its body wrapped in green-veined growths of a kind I have read about exactly once, in a margin note I cannot now locate.

I made no move toward it. Neither did anyone else.

That is where the day ends, in my notebook.
